Waakye

Waakye is a street food made with rice and beans boiled with a special waakye leaf that lends its color to the meal.

The meal is usually served in plantain leaves.

It is a staple in almost every Ghanaian home and can be eaten at any time of the day, either for breakfast, lunch, or supper.

It is undoubtedly Ghanaâ€™s favorite breakfast.

Delicious waakye served with all sides

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried black eyed beans/ peas soaked overnight
  • 2 cups rice
  • 8-10 dried millet stalk leaves
  • water for cooking
  • salt as required

Instructions

  1. Pour over a litre of water into a saucepan together with the millet leaf stalks.

  2. Put on high heat to boil. The millet stalk leaves will start omitting its colour and the water will start turning into a deep wine colour.

  3. Rinse the soaked beans and add to the boiling water. Cover and let it cook till tender. Mean while rinse the rice till the water is cleared.

  4. Add the washed rice into the saucepan together with salt to taste. (You might need to add more water)

  5. Add water if needed. Stir and cover tightly. Do not remove the millet stalk leaves.

  6. When the rice boils over, reduce heat and simmer till the water is absorbed and the rice is cooked through.

  7. Rest for three to five minutes, remove millet stalk leaves and fluff with a fork.
